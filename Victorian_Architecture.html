Victorian Architecture

Victorian architecture was known for rich decorative details and diversity of styles.Palaces and Victorian houses often featured arched windows, ornate columns, and small balconies with intricate designs.Different materials were used, including brick, natural stone, and carved wood, to display luxury and uniqueness.Prominent architectural styles included Neo-Gothic, Italianate, and Classical, each with distinctive features reflecting the refined taste of Victorian society.Architecture was not just construction; it was an artistic masterpiece reflecting social status and cultural wealth.
